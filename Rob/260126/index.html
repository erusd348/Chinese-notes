有 vs. 下雪yǒu vs. xià xuě有 + [weather phenomenon] indicates the existence of a weather condition. 下雪 is the verb "to snow".
我们城市有大雪。Wǒmen chéngshì yǒu dà xuě.Our city 有 heavy snow. (Describes a snowy condition)
我们城市下大雪了。Wǒmen chéngshì xià dà xuě le.It's snowing heavily in our city. (The snowfall event is happening/happened)
我们城市在下大雪。Wǒmen chéngshì zài xià dà xuě.It is snowing heavily in our city (right now).
[Time] 的时候[shíjiān] de shíhouMeans "when..." or "at the time of..."
你在大学的时候学习什么？Nǐ zài dàxué de shíhou xuéxí shénme?What did you study when you were in university?
我在大学的时候我学数学和德语。Wǒ zài dàxué de shíhou wǒ xué shùxué hé Déyǔ.When I was in university, I studied mathematics and German.
你最喜欢谁？Nǐ zuì xǐhuan shuí?Who do you like the most?
我最喜欢我的德语老师。Wǒ zuì xǐhuan wǒ de Déyǔ lǎoshī.I like my German teacher the most.
他们都是我的老师。Tāmen dōu shì wǒ de lǎoshī.They are all my teachers.
Address & Room Number Formatménpái hào géshìThe format for stating an address or room number in Chinese is from largest to smallest unit.
我住在1号楼2楼201室。Wǒ zhù zài yī hào lóu èr lóu èr líng yī shì.I live in Building 1, 2nd Floor, Room 201.
你的房间号是多少？Nǐ de fángjiān hào shì duōshao?What is your room number?
我的房间号是201。Wǒ de fángjiān hào shì èr líng yī.My room number is 201.
你住在多少号房间？Nǐ zhù zài duōshao hào fángjiān?What number room do you live in?
我住在201（号房间）。Wǒ zhù zài èr líng yī (hào fángjiān).I live in (room number) 201.
不要说话！Búyào shuōhuà!Don't talk! / Be quiet!
他很喜欢说话。Tā hěn xǐhuan shuōhuà.He really likes to talk. / He is very talkative.
我给我妈打电话。Wǒ gěi wǒ mā dǎ diànhuà.I call my mom. / I'm calling my mom.
Numbers in Phone Numbersdiànhuà hàomǎ lǐ de shùzìIn phone numbers, "1" is often pronounced as "yāo" for clarity to avoid confusion with "7" (qī).
我的电话号（码）是1（yao）23456。Wǒ de diànhuà hào(mǎ) shì yāo èr sān sì wǔ lìu.My phone number is 123456.
📚 Grammar
Grammar
在 + Verb
Indicates an action is currently in progress.
最 + Adjective / Verb
Forms the superlative degree, meaning "the most..."
都 + 是
Means "all are..." or "both are..."
不要 + Verb
Imperative negative: "Don't (do something)."
很 + Adjective / Verb
很 is often used before adjectives/stative verbs. It can mean "very" or just serve as a linking word.
给 + [Person] + Verb
Means "to do [verb] for/to [person]".
