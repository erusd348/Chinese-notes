📚 Grammar
Adv + V — Adverb + Verb
Use an adverb before a verb to modify the action. Common adverbs: 很 (very), 不/没 (not), 好 (well), etc.
很谢谢你。hěn xiè xie nǐ.Thank you very much.
不用谢！bú yòng xiè!You're welcome!
我没有老师。wǒ méi yǒu lǎo shī.I don't have a teacher.
我看不起你。wǒ kàn bu qǐ nǐ.I look down on you.
S + Adv + Adj — Subject + Adverb + Adjective
Chinese adjectives function like verbs. Place an adverb (很, 不, etc.) between the subject and adjective.
你很老。nǐ hěn lǎo.You are very old.
你很好。nǐ hěn hǎo.You are very good.
Android 很好用。Android hěn hǎo yòng.Android is very good to use.
你很好看。nǐ hěn hǎo kàn.You are very good looking.
我们关系很好。wǒ men guān xi hěn hǎo.Our relationship is good.
我们关系不好。wǒ men guān xi bù hǎo.Our relationship is not good.
请 + V — Polite Requests
Place 请 before a verb to make a polite request, similar to "please + verb" in English.
请进。qǐng jìn.Please come in.
请坐。qǐng zuò.Please sit down.
请听。qǐng tīng.Please listen.
请说。qǐng shuō.Please speak.
请读。qǐng dú.Please read.
请写。qǐng xiě.Please write.
