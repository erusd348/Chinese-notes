💬 Dialogue
A: 你是哪国人？Nǐ shì nǎ guó rénWhich country are you from?
B: 我是美国人。Wǒ shì Měiguó rénI am American.
A: 您贵姓？Nín guìxìngMay I ask your surname?
B: 我姓王。Wǒ xìng WángMy surname is Wang.
📝 Grammar
哪 — Asking "Which"
哪 (nǎ) + noun = "which (noun)?" Often used with 国 (country), 个 (general), 位 (polite for people).
• 你是哪国人？   Nǐ shì nǎ guó rén?   Which country are you from?
• 你是哪国人？   Nǐ shì nǎ guó rén?   What's your nationality?
几 — Asking "How Many" (Small Numbers)
几 (jǐ) asks about small numbers (usually < 10). For larger quantities, use 多少.
• 你有几位老师？   Nǐ yǒu jǐ wèi lǎoshī?   How many teachers do you have?
• 你住几号房间？   Nǐ zhù jǐ hào fángjiān?   Which room number do you live in?
贵姓 — Polite Forms
贵姓 (guìxìng) is the polite way to ask someone's surname. Literally "honorable surname." The answer is just the surname — no 的 or 我 needed.
• 您贵姓？   Nín guìxìng?   What's your (honorable) surname?
• 我姓李。   Wǒ xìng Lǐ.   My surname is Li.
