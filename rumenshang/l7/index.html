📝 Grammar
S + (S + Predicate) — Topic-Comment Structure
A noun comes first as the topic, followed by a full subject-predicate comment about it. Common with body parts and personal attributes.
• 你身体好吗？   Nǐ shēntǐ hǎo ma?   How's your health? (Lit: You — body good?)
• 我身体很好。   Wǒ shēntǐ hěn hǎo.   My health is good.
• 他个子很高。   Tā gèzi hěn gāo.   He is tall. (Lit: He — height very tall.)
有 / 没有 — Having & Not Having
有 (yǒu) = have/there is. 没有 (méiyǒu) = don't have/there isn't. NEVER use 不 with 有.
• 宿舍里有空调吗？   Sùshè li yǒu kōngtiáo ma?   Is there AC in the dorm?
• 有。   Yǒu.   Yes, there is.
• 没有空调。   Méiyǒu kōngtiáo.   There is no AC.
A-不-A — Affirmative-Negative Questions
Repeat the verb/adjective with 不 in the middle to form a question. This is another way to ask yes/no questions (besides 吗).
• 你忙不忙？   Nǐ máng bu máng?   Are you busy (or not)?
• 学校远不远？   Xuéxiào yuǎn bu yuǎn?   Is the school far?
