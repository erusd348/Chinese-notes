💬 Dialogue
我要换钱。Wǒ yào huàn qián.I want to exchange money.
你要换多少钱？Nǐ yào huàn duōshǎo qián?How much do you want to exchange?
我要换一百美元。Wǒ yào huàn yìbǎi měiyuán.I want to exchange $100.

📝 Grammar
Numbers 1–10
Chinese numbers are regular: 十一 (11), 二十 (20), 二十五 (25). Count: 一、二、三、四、五、六、七、八、九、十.
• 一、二、三、四、五   yī, èr, sān, sì, wǔ   1, 2, 3, 4, 5
• 六、七、八、九、十   liù, qī, bā, jiǔ, shí   6, 7, 8, 9, 10
二 (èr) vs. 两 (liǎng) — Two
Use 二 for counting and in compound numbers (十二, 二十). Use 两 BEFORE measure words: 两个, 两块, 两杯.
• 两个杯子   liǎng gè bēizi   two cups
• 两杯咖啡   liǎng bēi kāfēi   two cups of coffee
• 十二块钱   shí'èr kuài qián   twelve kuai
多少 — Asking "How Much/Many"
多少 (duōshǎo) asks about quantity (usually ≥10). Place it before the noun.
• 多少钱？   Duōshǎo qián?   How much money?
• 你要换多少？   Nǐ yào huàn duōshǎo?   How much do you want to exchange?
Chinese Money (RMB)
Basic units: 块 (kuài, yuan), 毛 (máo, 1/10 yuan), 分 (fēn, 1/100 yuan). Price format: X块X毛X分.
• 三块五毛   sān kuài wǔ máo   3.50 yuan
• 十九块四毛   shíjiǔ kuài sì máo   19.40 yuan
• 三十块零六毛   sānshí kuài líng liù máo   30.60 yuan
• 五十块   wǔshí kuài   50 yuan
