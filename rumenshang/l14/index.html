💬 Dialogue
A: 咱们去尝尝，好吗？Zánmen qù cháng chang, hǎo maLet's go taste it, OK?
B: 好，走吧。Hǎo, zǒu baOK, let’s go.
📝 Grammar
咱们 vs. 我们 — Inclusive vs. Exclusive "We"
咱们 (zánmen) includes the listener: "you and I." 我们 (wǒmen) may or may not include the listener — it can mean "we (but not you)."
• 咱们去尝尝，好吗？   Zánmen qù chángchang, hǎo ma?   Let's go taste it, OK? (you and I)
• 我们去吃饭。   Wǒmen qù chīfàn.   We go eat. (may or may not include the listener)
好吗 / 吧 — Making Suggestions
好吗 at end = "OK?" (soft suggestion). 吧 at end = "let's…" (more decisive). Both turn statements into suggestions.
• 咱们去尝尝，好吗？   Zánmen qù chángchang, hǎo ma?   Let's go taste it, OK?
• 我们走吧。   Wǒmen zǒu ba.   Let's go.
最 — The Superlative "Most"
最 (zuì) + adjective = "the most (adj)," "-est." Simple and direct — no comparison marker needed.
• 最好吃   zuì hǎochī   the most delicious
• 最喜欢   zuì xǐhuān   like the most / favorite
不过 — "However / But"
不过 (búguò) is softer than 但是 (dànshì). Used in spoken Chinese to introduce a mild contrast or reservation.
• 很好吃，不过有点儿油。   Hěn hǎochī, búguò yǒudiǎnr yóu.   Very tasty, but a bit oily.
• 我喜欢，不过太辣了。   Wǒ xǐhuān, búguò tài là le.   I like it, but it's too spicy.
