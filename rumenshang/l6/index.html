📝 Grammar
是 — The "To Be" Verb (Identification)
是 (shì) connects two nouns/noun phrases to show identity: "A is B". For adjectives, use 很 instead — do NOT use 是 with adjectives.
• 我是留学生。   Wǒ shì liúxuéshēng.   I am an international student.
• 这是我的老师。   Zhè shì wǒmen de lǎoshī.   This is our teacher.
的 — Possession & Modification
的 (de) shows possession: A + 的 + B = "A's B" or "B of A". With close family/friends or institutions, 的 can be dropped.
• 我们的教室   wǒmen de jiàoshì   our classroom
• 我的汉语书   wǒ de Hànyǔ shū   my Chinese book
• 我同学的词典   wǒ tóngxué de cídiǎn   my classmate's dictionary
很 + Adjective — Describing with Adjectives
In Chinese, adjectives don't need 是. Use 很 (very) before the adjective. Even when you don't mean "very," 很 is often used as a linker.
• 她很漂亮。   Tā hěn piàoliang.   She is pretty.
• 他很帅。   Tā hěn shuài.   He is handsome.
• 我们的教室不大。   Wǒmen de jiàoshì bú dà.   Our classroom is not big.
