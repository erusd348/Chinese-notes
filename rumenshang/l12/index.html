💬 Dialogue
A: 你要红的还是要蓝的？Nǐ yào hóng de háishi yào lán deDo you want the red one or the blue one?
B: 我要红的。Wǒ yào hóng deI want the red one.
📝 Grammar
还是 — Choice Questions ("Or")
还是 (háishì) is used for "or" in QUESTIONS only. Answer by choosing one option. In statements, use 或者 (huòzhě) instead.
• 要红的还是要蓝的？   Yào hóng de háishì yào lán de?   Do you want the red one or the blue one?
• 你喝茶还是喝咖啡？   Nǐ hē chá háishì hē kāfēi?   Do you drink tea or coffee?
的 — Nominalization (Turning Adjectives into Nouns)
Adjective + 的 turns the adjective into a noun phrase: 红的 = "the red one," 甜的 = "the sweet one."
• 红的   hóng de   the red one
• 甜的   tián de   the sweet one
• 别的   bié de   something else / others
了 — Completed Action Marker
了 (le) after a verb indicates that the action is completed. It's about completion, not past tense.
• 我买了三个苹果。   Wǒ mǎi le sān gè píngguǒ.   I bought three apples.
• 你买了什么？   Nǐ mǎi le shénme?   What did you buy?
Adjectives for Taste & Quality
Common descriptive adjectives used for food, drinks, and things. Remember: Adj + 的 nominalizes them.
• 甜、酸、辣、凉、热   tián, suān, là, liáng, rè   sweet, sour, spicy, cool, hot
• 新鲜、好吃   xīnxiān, hǎochī   fresh, delicious
