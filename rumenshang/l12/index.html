💬 Dialogue
A: 买两支圆珠笔。Mǎi liǎng zhī yuánzhūbǐ.I'd like two ballpoint pens.
B: 要红的还是要蓝的？Yào hóng de háishì yào lán de?Do you want the red one or the blue one?
A: 一样一支。Yī yàng yī zhī.One of each.
B: 还要别的吗？Hái yào bié de ma?Anything else?
A: 不要了。Bú yào le.That's all.
A: 请问，哪儿卖啤酒？Qǐngwèn, nǎr mài píjiǔ?Excuse me, where can I buy beer?
B: 那儿卖。Nàr mài.Over there.
A: 有青岛啤酒吗？Yǒu Qīngdǎo píjiǔ ma?Do you have Tsingtao beer?
B: 有，要几瓶？Yǒu, yào jǐ píng?Yes, how many bottles?
A: 要四瓶，再要两听可口可乐。Yào sì píng, zài yào liǎng tīng Kěkǒukělè.Four bottles, and two cans of Coca-Cola.
A: 一共多少钱？Yīgòng duōshǎo qián?How much in total?
B: 十九块四。Shíjiǔ kuài sì.19.40 yuan.
A: 给你钱。Gěi nǐ qián.Here's the money.
B: 您有零钱吗？Nín yǒu língqián ma?Do you have change?
A: 没有。Méiyǒu.No.
B: 收您五十块，找您三十块零六毛。请数一下儿。Shōu nín wǔshí kuài, zhǎo nín sānshí kuài líng liù máo. Qǐng shǔ yīxiàr.Received 50 yuan — your change is 30.60. Please count it.
A: 橘子多少钱一斤？Júzi duōshǎo qián yī jīn?How much per jin for the oranges?
B: 大的三块钱一斤，小的十块钱四斤。Dà de sān kuài qián yī jīn, xiǎo de shí kuài qián sì jīn.Big ones 3 yuan/jin, small ones 10 yuan for 4 jin.
A: 甜不甜？Tián bu tián?Are they sweet?
B: 您尝一下儿，不甜不要钱。Nín cháng yīxiàr, bù tián bú yào qián.Taste one — if not sweet, no charge.
A: 西红柿怎么卖？Xīhóngshì zěnme mài?How do you sell the tomatoes?
B: 一斤三块八。Yī jīn sān kuài bā.3.80 yuan per jin.
A: 新鲜不新鲜？Xīnxiān bu xīnxiān?Are they fresh?
B: 这是今天早上摘的，新鲜极了。Zhè shì jīntiān zǎoshang zhāi de, xīnxiān jí le.These were picked this morning — extremely fresh.
📝 Grammar
还是 — Choice Questions ("Or")
还是 is "or" in questions only. Answer by picking one. In statements, use 或者.
• 要红的还是要蓝的？   Yào hóng de háishì yào lán de?   Do you want red or blue?
• 一样一支。   Yī yàng yī zhī.   One of each.
的 — Nominalization ("the...one")
Adj + 的 turns an adjective into a noun: 红的 = "the red one", 大的 = "the big one", 别的 = "other things".
• 红的   hóng de   the red one
• 大的   dà de   the big one(s)
• 还要别的吗？   Hái yào bié de ma?   Anything else?
了 — Change of Situation / New Circumstance
At sentence end, 了 marks a change or new situation — NOT completion. It signals something is different now.
• 不要了。   Bú yào le.   (I) don't want (anymore).
• 不吃了。   Bù chī le.   (I'm) not eating (anymore).
• 太贵了。   Tài guì le.   (It's) too expensive. (change from expectation)
A-不-A — Asking About Quality
Use A-不-A pattern with adjectives to ask about quality: 甜不甜？ 新鲜不新鲜？
• 甜不甜？   Tián bu tián?   Is it sweet?
• 新鲜不新鲜？   Xīnxiān bu xīnxiān?   Is it fresh?
• 不甜不要钱。   Bù tián bú yào qián.   If not sweet, no charge.
