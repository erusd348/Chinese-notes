📝 Grammar
Basic Greetings & Responses
Chinese uses set phrases for greetings. 你好 (nǐ hǎo) literally means "you good" — a simple greeting pattern. Responses often mirror the greeting or use fixed polite forms.
• 你好！   Nǐ hǎo!   Hello!
• 老师好！   Lǎoshī hǎo!   Hello, teacher!
Polite Phrase Pairs
Chinese has paired polite expressions: 谢谢 ↔ 不客气, 对不起 ↔ 没关系. Learn them as fixed pairs.
• A: 谢谢！ B: 不客气。   A: Xièxie! B: Bú kèqì.   A: Thanks! B: You're welcome.
• A: 对不起！ B: 没关系。   A: Duìbuqǐ! B: Méi guānxi.   A: Sorry! B: It's OK.
请 + Verb — Polite Requests
Put 请 before a verb to make a polite request or invitation.
• 请进。   Qǐng jìn.   Please come in.
• 请坐。   Qǐng zuò.   Please sit down.
