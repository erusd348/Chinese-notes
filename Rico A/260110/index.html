📚 Grammar
A 最 + Adj.
Superlative “A is the most...”: Used to state that A possesses the highest degree of a quality among a group (explicit or implied).
A 离 B (Adv. +) Adj.
Structure “Distance from A to B is (Adv.) Adj.”: Describes the distance between two places (A and B). The adjective is usually 远 (far) or 近 (near). Adverbs like 很， 非常， 不， 最 can modify the adjective.
A 比 B + Adj.
Comparative “A is more Adj. than B.”: Used to compare two things (A and B), stating that A has a higher degree of the adjective quality than B.
B 没有 A (这么/那么) + Adj.
Negative Comparative “B is not as Adj. as A.”: A less direct or negative way to compare, meaning B does not reach the level of A. 这么/那么 (this/that much) can be added for emphasis.
V./Adj. + 起来
Structure “When V./Adj.-ed” or “Looks/Feels...”: 1) Indicates the start of an action or a state. 2) When used with perception verbs like 看/听/尝 (look/sound/taste), it means “looks/sounds/tastes...”.
