📝 Notes
这儿 / 那儿 — Location Words with People/Places
The demonstrative pronouns 这儿 (here) and 那儿 (there) can follow a personal pronoun or a non-place noun (e.g., a company name) to indicate location. ……这儿 means 'near the speaker' and ……那儿 means 'away from the speaker.'
Example from the text: 刚才我去他那儿的时候 — 'When I went to his place just now.' Here, 他那儿 refers to 'his location.'
📚 Grammar
The Progressive Aspect (动作的进行)
To show an action in progress, place「正在」「正」or「在」before the verb, or add「呢」at the end. The negative uses「没(有)」or「没在」. This works for present, past, and future times.
他正在等他爱人呢。tā zhèng zài děng tā ài rén ne。He is waiting for his wife.
外面正下大雨呢。wài miàn zhèng xià dà yǔ ne。It's raining hard outside.
他在睡觉呢。tā shuì jiào ne。He's sleeping (in bed).
有的……有的…… — 'Some... Some...'
「有的」points to part of a group. It can be repeated to describe different parts of the same group.
商店里顾客很多，有的正在挑东西，有的正在排队交钱。shāng diàn lǐ gù kè hěn duō， yǒu de zhèng zài tiāo dōng xī， yǒu de zhèng zài pái duì jiāo qián。There are many customers in the store — some are picking items, some are queuing to pay.
一边……一边…… — 'Doing Two Things at Once'
Used before two verbs to show two actions happening simultaneously.
莉莉跟朋友们一边喝咖啡一边聊天儿。lì lì gēn péng yǒu men yī biān hē kā fēi yī biān liáo tiān ér。Lily chats with friends while drinking coffee.
他一边听音乐一边看书。tā yī biān tīng yīn yuè yī biān kàn shū。He reads while listening to music.
