Dialogue 1
这是英男和保罗的房间，门开着，电视也开着。zhè shì yīng nán hé bǎo luó de fáng jiān， mén kāi zhe， diàn shì yě kāi zhe。This is Yingnan and Paul's room. The door is open and the TV is on.
书 和词典在书架上放着，保罗在椅子上坐着，英男在床上躺着，他们正在紧张地看着电视里的足球赛。shū hé cí diǎn zài shū jià shàng fàng zhe， bǎo luó zài yǐ zi shàng zuò zhe， yīng nán zài chuáng shàng tǎng zhe， tā men zhèng zài jǐn zhāng dì kàn zhe diàn shì lǐ de zú qiú sài。Books and dictionaries are on the shelf. 📝 Notes
太……了 — High Degree
太……了 indicates a high degree. E.g., 太好了 — 'Great!'
麻烦您…… — Asking for Help
麻烦您…… is a common polite way to ask someone for help. E.g., 麻烦您转告他一件事 — 'Could you please pass a message to him?'
没问题 — 'No Problem'
没问题 is used to respond to a question or request, confirming that you can do it.
📚 Grammar
着 — Ongoing State / Continuation
The particle「着」after a verb indicates a continuous state or an ongoing action. Pattern: Verb + 着 + (Object) — describing how things are arranged or how people are positioned.
门开着，电视也开着。mén kāi zhe， diàn shì yě kāi zhe。The door is open and the TV is on.
书在书架上放着。shū zài shū jià shàng fàng zhe。Books are placed on the shelf.
他在椅子上坐着。tā zài yǐ zi shàng zuò zhe。He's sitting on the chair.
Existential Sentences — 有 / 是
To describe where something exists, use「处所词 + Verb + 着 + 物」. For existence,「V+着」often replaces「有」when describing how things are arranged.
门口挂着一个牌子。mén kǒu guà zhe yí gè pái zi。A sign hangs at the entrance.
桌子上摆着各种东西。zhuō zi shàng bǎi zhe gè zhǒng dōng xī。All kinds of things are placed on the table.
窗台上趴着一只小猫。chuāng tái shàng pā zhe yī zhī xiǎo māo。A little cat is lying on the windowsill.
的 — Nominalizing with 的
「的」can turn a verb phrase into a noun phrase, meaning "the one that..." This is like a relative clause without a head noun.
吃的和用的东西chī de hé yòng de dōng xīThings to eat and things to use
