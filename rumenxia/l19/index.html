Dialogue 1
直美：你会游泳吗 ?zhí měi： nǐ huì yóu yǒng ma?Naomi: Can you swim?
小叶：我会游泳。xiǎo yè： wǒ huì yóu yǒng。Xiaoye: Yes, I can swim.
直美：你游泳游得怎么样 ?zhí měi： nǐ yóu yǒng yóu dé zěn me yàng?Naomi: How well can you swim?
小叶：我游得不错，能游八百米呢 。xiǎo yè： wǒ yóu dé bù cuò， néng yóu bā bǎi mǐ ne。Xiaoye: I swim pretty well — I can swim 800 meters.
直美：我真羡慕你，我是个旱鸭子。zhí měi： wǒ zhēn xiàn mù nǐ， wǒ shì gè hàn yā zi。Naomi: I really envy you. I'm a landlubber (can't swim at all).

📝 Notes
呢 for Exaggeration & Pride
In 能游八百米呢 (I can swim 800 meters), the particle 呢 emphasizes the fact while adding a slight sense of exaggeration and pride.
不怎么样 — 'Not So Great'
不怎么样 expresses a negative evaluation. It has the same meaning as 不好 (not good). E.g., 踢得不怎么样 — '(He) doesn't play very well.'
📚 Grammar
Complement of State — V + 得 + Adj
The particle「得」connects a verb to a complement describing the state or result of the action. Pattern: Verb + 得 + Adjective. For questions: Verb + 得 + Adj + 吗? or Verb + 得 + 怎么样?
你游泳游得怎么样？nǐ yóu yǒng yóu dé zěn me yàng？How well do you swim?
我游得不错。wǒ yóu dé bù cuò。I swim pretty well.
他踢得不怎么样。tā tī dé bù zěn me yàng。He doesn't play very well.
The 不 V 不? Pattern — A-not-A Questions
To ask a yes-no question with a predicate, use the A-not-A form:「Verb + 不 + Verb?」Or for longer verbs:「V + 不 + V + Object?」.
你会不会踢足球？nǐ huì bú huì tī zú qiú？Can you play soccer or not?
你想不想去看电影？nǐ xiǎng bù xiǎng qù kàn diàn yǐng？Do you want to go see a movie or not?
