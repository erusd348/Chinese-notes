💬 Dialogue
课文 1
张英：季红，今天咱俩去哪儿玩儿?
季红：有名的地方差不多都去过了，只有香山和天坛公园还没去。
张英：香山比天坛近一些，我建议今天去香山。
季红：行啊。 那咱们怎么去呢?
张英：坐公交或者骑车都行，你说吧。
季红：坐公交太挤了。这几天我天天坐，已经坐腻了。
张英：不想坐公交，那咱们就骑车去。
季红：骑车去有点儿远，而且还有山路。
张英：没关系，要是累了， 咱们就停下来歇会儿。
季红：去可能没问题，回来的时候就很难说啦。
张英：要是骑不动了，咱们就在路上找个饭馆儿先吃饭。

望月：坐公共汽车吧。去香山坐几路车啊?
爱珍：我用手机上网查查地图，咱们一起看看。

望月：那是因为故宫在市区，沿途都是热闹的地方，所以很容易堵车。
课文 3
飞龙的朋友黄勇邀请飞龙周末去他家里玩儿。黄勇的家在郊区，骑车去有点儿远。再说，7飞龙骑车的技术不高，他不敢骑车去。坐公共汽车也不方便，得倒好几次车。打车呢? 几十公里， 太贵，而且路上可能会堵车。飞龙上网查了一下地图，发现有一条刚刚通车的地铁线路就到黄勇家附近，出了地铁站走路只要十分钟。最后飞龙决定坐地铁去黄勇家做客。
📝 Notes
**[1] 差不多 (chàbuduō) — almost; nearly; about the same**
"差不多" means "almost", "nearly", or "about the same". It indicates a small difference between two things — they are close but not exactly the same. It can also be used before quantities.
① 咱们俩差不多高。 (The two of us are about the same height.)
② 这两个餐厅的价格差不多。 (The prices at these two restaurants are about the same.)
③ 教室里坐了差不多20人。 (There are almost 20 people sitting in the classroom.)
**[2] 比 (bǐ) — comparative structure**
The preposition "比" is used to make comparisons: A + 比 + B + Adjective + (Difference). The "Difference" can be a quantity or words like "得多", "多了", "一点儿".
① 香山比天坛近一些。 (Fragrant Hills is a bit closer than the Temple of Heaven.)
② 他比我高得多。 (He is much taller than me.)
③ 今天比昨天冷多了。 (Today is much colder than yesterday.)
**[3] 腻 (nì) — to be tired of; fed up with**
"腻" describes the feeling of being tired of something because you have done it too many times or had too much of it. Commonly used for food, activities, or entertainment.
① 天天吃方便面，我都吃腻了。 (Eating instant noodles every day — Im sick of them.)
② 这首歌我听了好多遍，都听腻了。 (Ive listened to this song so many times — Im tired of it.)
③ 同一个电影看三遍，你不腻吗？ (Watching the same movie three times — dont you get tired of it?)
**[4] 要是 (yàoshì) — if; in case (hypothetical)**
"要是" is a conjunction used in the first clause to introduce a hypothesis. "就", "那", or "那么" often appears in the second clause to introduce the result.
① 明天要是下雨，我们就不去了。 (If it rains tomorrow, we wont go.)
② 要是你不来，那我也不去了。 (If youre not coming, then Im not going either.)
③ 要是你有时间，我们就一起去。 (If you have time, lets go together.)
**[5] 千万 (qiānwàn) — must; be sure to; by all means**
"千万" expresses "must" or "be sure to". It is used when urging someone earnestly or expressing a strong wish. Usually followed by "要" or a negative expression.
① 明天的晚会你千万要来呀。 (You absolutely must come to tomorrows party.)
② 你千万别忘了带护照。 (Dont forget to bring your passport — whatever you do!)
③ 千万别告诉他这个消息。 (Be sure NOT to tell him this news.)
**[6] 因为……所以…… (yīnwèi...suǒyǐ...) — because...therefore...**
The most common structure for expressing cause and effect. "因为" introduces the reason, and "所以" introduces the result.
① 那是因为故宫在市区，沿途都是热闹的地方，所以很容易堵车。 (Thats because the Forbidden City is downtown and the route is full of busy areas, so traffic jams are common.)
② 因为下雨，所以比赛取消了。 (Because it rained, the match was cancelled.)
③ 因为他很努力，所以进步很快。 (Because he works hard, hes improving quickly.)
**[7] 再说 (zàishuō) — moreover; besides; furthermore**
"再说" (as a conjunction) is used in spoken Chinese to give a further reason or add new supporting arguments. It usually appears at the beginning of the second sentence.
① 再说，飞龙骑车的技术不高，他不敢骑车去。 (Besides, Feilongs cycling skills arent good — he doesnt dare to ride there.)
② 这本书没什么意思，再说你已经有一本差不多的书了，别买了。 (This book isnt interesting — besides, you already have a similar one. Dont buy it.)
③ 我们是好朋友，不用客气。再说，互相帮忙是应该的。 (Were good friends — no need to be polite. Besides, helping each other is only right.)
**[8] 再 + 说 (zài + shuō) — to talk about it later**
"再 + 说" literally means "to talk (about it) later". One doesnt want to do or decide something now, but will do so when the right time comes.
① 先找工作吧，其他的事以后再说。 (Find a job first — well talk about other things later.)
② 今天太晚了，明天再说吧。 (Its too late today. Lets talk about it tomorrow.)
③ 你先找地方住下，工作的事过几天再说。 (Find a place to stay first — well talk about work in a few days.)
NB: Note the difference from "再说" (conjunction, meaning "besides/moreover") vs. "再+说" (adverb+verb, meaning "talk about later").
