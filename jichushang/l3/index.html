课文 3
以前中国大学生的学习生活非常简单，可以说是“三点一线”——从宿舍到教室、图书馆，从教室、图书馆到食堂，再从食堂到宿舍。可是，现在大学生的生活越来越丰富了，酒吧、咖啡厅、健身房都是他们常去的地方。他们不但很会玩儿，而且学习也都非常努力。他们努力学习，为的是以后能找到一个好工作。他们“努力”玩儿，是因为工作以后就没时间玩儿了。
📝 Notes
**[1] (你)这是……? — asking what someone is doing**
"(你)这是……?" is a common way to ask what someone is currently doing. It often carries a tone of curiosity or friendly inquiry.
① 你这是去哪儿？ (Where are you going?)
② 你这是干什么？ (What are you doing?)
③ 你这是怎么了？ (Whats wrong with you? / What happened?)
**[2] 原来……怪不得…… (yuánlái...guàibude...) — now I understand why...**
This pair expresses a sudden realization. "原来" introduces the reason, and "怪不得" introduces the situation that now makes sense. The speaker finally understands something that was puzzling before.
① 原来是这样，怪不得这几天我经常看见你在校园里转。 (So thats it — no wonder Ive been seeing you around campus these days.)
② 原来他病了，怪不得没来上课。 (So he was sick — no wonder he didnt come to class.)
③ 原来她是中国人，怪不得汉语说得这么好。 (So shes Chinese — no wonder she speaks Chinese so well.)
**[3] 什么 (shénme) — "whatever" meaning (not a question)**
"什么" can be used not as a question word, but to mean "whatever" or "any". In this usage, it is usually followed by "都" or "也" for emphasis.
① "什么" here doesnt ask what there is — it means "whatever you need, the campus has it".
② 他什么都吃。 (He eats everything.)
③ 我什么都不知道。 (I dont know anything.)
④ 今天他去商店了，可是什么都没买。 (He went to the store today but didnt buy anything.)
Other question words like "谁", "哪儿" can be used in the same pattern.
**[4] 这么 + Adj — to such a degree; so...**
"这么" (or "那么") can be placed before an adjective or auxiliary verb to indicate a high degree. It adds vividness and often carries a slight sense of surprise or exaggeration.
① 想不到你已经这么熟悉了！ (I didnt expect you to be THIS familiar already!)
② 想不到北京的夏天这么热！ (I never expected Beijings summer to be THIS hot!)
③ 生词这么多，今天学不完了。 (There are so many new words — we cant finish learning them today.)
**[5] 不但……而且…… (búdàn...érqiě...) — not only...but also...**
In the pattern "不但……而且……", the clause after "而且" adds further information that goes beyond the first clause or provides additional details. When the subject is the same, it goes BEFORE "不但".
① 里边不但有吃的、喝的，而且有穿的、用的，东西很全。 (Not only are there things to eat and drink, but also clothes and daily necessities — its very complete.)
② 她不但喜欢唱歌，而且唱得很好。 (She not only likes singing, but also sings well.)
③ 我不但去过故宫，而且去过三次。 (Ive not only been to the Forbidden City, but Ive been three times.)
**[6] 为的是 (wèi de shì) — the purpose is to; in order to**
"为的是" works the same as "是为了" to indicate the purpose or aim of doing something. It is placed after stating the action, explaining why it was done.
① 他们努力学习，为的是以后能找到一个好工作。 (They study hard in order to find a good job in the future.)
② 大家现在努力工作，为的是以后生活得更好。 (Everyone works hard now so that life will be better in the future.)
③ 麦克努力工作，为的是挣更多的钱买房、买车。 (Mike works hard to earn more money to buy a house and a car.)
