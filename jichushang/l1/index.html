📝 Notes
**[1] 越来越 (yuèláiyuè) — more and more**
"越来越" means "more and more" or "increasingly". It indicates that the degree of something grows over time. It is followed by an adjective or a verb that expresses a change.
① 天气越来越凉快。 (The weather is getting cooler and cooler.)
② 汉语越来越难。 (Chinese is getting harder and harder.)
③ 他越来越喜欢中国。 (He likes China more and more.)
**[2] 是……的 (shì...de) — emphasizing time, place, manner, or agent**
"是……的" is used to emphasize the time, place, manner, or person who did something. The action must be something that has already happened.
Structure: 是 + [emphasized part] + Verb + 的
① 他是今年3月到北京的。 (emphasizes time → He arrived in Beijing in March this year.)
② 这本书是在外文书店买的。 (emphasizes place → I bought this book at the Foreign Language Bookstore.)
③ 教室里的灯不是田中开的，是飞龙开的。 (emphasizes agent → It was Feilong who turned on the light, not Tanaka.)
**[3] 先……然后…… (xiān...ránhòu...) — first...then...**
"先" and "然后" work together to show the sequence of two events. The first clause (with 先) happens first, and the second clause (with 然后) happens after.
① 我要先在这儿学习半年，然后在中国工作。 (I will first study here for six months, then work in China.)
② 先吃饭，然后去看电影。 (Eat first, then go see a movie.)
③ 你先打电话问问，然后再决定。 (Call and ask first, then decide.)
**[4] 一边……一边…… (yìbiān...yìbiān...) — doing two things at the same time**
Two "一边" are used together to indicate doing two actions at the same time. The verbs must be actions that the person can control.
① 妈妈一边做饭，一边跟客人聊天儿。 (Mom chats with the guest while cooking.)
② 飞龙一边弹吉他，一边唱歌。 (Feilong sings while playing the guitar.)
③ 不要一边走路一边看手机。 (Dont look at your phone while walking.)
NB: The verbs must be voluntary actions. It would be wrong to say "他一边受表扬，一边脸红了" since receiving praise and blushing are not both voluntary.
**[5] 一……也/都+不/没…… — emphasizing total negation**
This structure is used to emphasize a strong negation. "一" is followed by a measure word (with or without a noun). "也" or "都" can be used before "不" or "没".
① 马克来中国以前没学过汉语，一个汉字也/都不认识。 (Mark had never studied Chinese before coming to China — he didnt know a single character.)
② 我一口也没吃。 (I didnt eat even one bite.)
③ 教室里一个人都没有。 (There isnt a single person in the classroom.)
Optionally, "连" can be placed before "一" for extra emphasis.
**[6] 为了 (wèile) — in order to; for the purpose of**
"为了" is usually placed at the beginning of the first clause to indicate a purpose or goal. The second clause explains the action taken to achieve that goal.
① 为了让女儿专心工作，老人每天去女儿家里帮助做家务。 (In order to let his daughter focus on work, the old man goes to her home every day to help with housework.)
② 为了学好汉语，她每天练习两个小时。 (In order to learn Chinese well, she practices for two hours every day.)
Sometimes the action is stated first, followed by "是为了": 他每天跑步，是为了身体健康。 (He runs every day in order to be healthy.)
