📝 Notes
**[1] 才 (cái) — indicating a small quantity**
"才" is an adverb that indicates a small amount. When used before a verb + quantity, it means "only" or "merely", suggesting the speaker thinks the quantity is less than expected.
① 他一天才吃了两顿饭。 (He only ate two meals in a whole day — thats very little.)
② 我才学了三个月汉语。 (Ive only studied Chinese for three months.)
③ 这个房间才十平方米。 (This room is only ten square meters.)
**[2] 偏偏 (piānpiān) — contrary to expectations**
"偏偏" is an adverb indicating that something happens contrary to ones wishes or expectations. It often conveys disappointment or frustration.
① 下午我学习的时候，他偏偏要睡觉！ (In the afternoon when Im studying, he just has to sleep!)
② 好不容易到周末了，偏偏下起雨来。 (I finally made it to the weekend, and of course it had to rain.)
③ 大家都同意了，他偏偏反对。 (Everyone agreed, but he just had to oppose it.)
**[3] 既……也/又…… (jì...yě/yòu...) — both...and...; neither...nor...**
"既……也/又……" connects two verbs or adjectives to indicate that two qualities or situations exist simultaneously. Use "也" for a milder tone and "又" for a stronger one.
① 她既没说同意，也没说不同意。 (She neither agreed nor disagreed.)
② 他既聪明又努力。 (He is both smart and hardworking.)
③ 这件衣服既便宜又好看。 (This piece of clothing is both cheap and nice-looking.)
**[4] 能 (néng) vs. 会 (huì) — expressing ability**
"能" means "can" — having the ability, condition, or permission to do something. "会" means "know how to" — a skill learned through practice.
① 我会说汉语。 (I can speak Chinese — I know how to.)
② 我不能去，明天有事。 (I cant go — I have something to do tomorrow.)
③ 学了汉字才能看书。 (Only after learning characters can one read books.) [能 = objective possibility]
**[5] 怎么 + Verb + 得/不 + Complement — rhetorical "how can"**
"怎么" in this structure is a rhetorical question meaning "how can/how could". It implies the speaker believes something is impossible or unreasonable.
① 天天睡不好，怎么能有精神？ (If you cant sleep well every day, how can you have energy?)
② 不练习，怎么能学好汉语？ (If you dont practice, how can you learn Chinese well?)
③ 这么贵的手机，我怎么买得起？ (Such an expensive phone — how could I afford it?)
**[6] 不是……吗？(bú shì...ma) — rhetorical question**
"不是……吗？" is a rhetorical question pattern. The speaker uses it to point out something obvious, expecting agreement. It translates to "Isnt it...?" or "Dont you...?"
① 咱们的宿舍楼不是临街吗？ (Isnt our dormitory building facing the street? — It obviously is.)
② 你不是中国人吗？怎么不会用筷子？ (Arent you Chinese? How come you cant use chopsticks?)
③ 你不是说过要来吗？ (Didnt you say you would come?)
**[7] 或者……或者…… (huòzhě...huòzhě...) — either...or...**
"或者……或者……" presents two or more alternatives in declarative sentences. For questions, use "还是" instead.
① 你或者想办法换个房间，或者快点儿习惯新环境。 (Either find a way to change rooms, or quickly get used to the new environment.)
② 周末我或者看书，或者看电影。 (On weekends I either read or watch a movie.)
③ 你或者坐地铁去，或者打车去。 (You can either take the subway or take a taxi.)
**[8] 再说吧 (zàishuō ba) — lets talk about it later**
"再说吧" means not wanting to discuss or decide something now, but to deal with it later when the time is right. "再" means "later/further", "说" means "discuss".
① 今天太晚了，明天再说吧。 (Its too late today. Lets talk about it tomorrow.)
② A: 你打算什么时候结婚？ B: 以后再说吧。 (A: When do you plan to get married? B: Well see later.)
③ 你先找工作，其他的事以后再说。 (Find a job first; well worry about other things later.)
