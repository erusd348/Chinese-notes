📝 Notes
**[1] 以为 (yǐwéi) — to have thought (mistakenly)**
"以为" indicates someone made a judgment or assumption, but later found out it was wrong. Different from "认为" (to think/believe), which doesnt imply the judgment is wrong.
① 我以为他走了，原来他还在。 (I thought he had left, but he is still here.)
② 我以为今天星期二呢。 (I thought today was Tuesday.)
③ 别以为学习汉语很容易，其实挺难的。 (Dont think learning Chinese is easy — it is actually quite hard.)
**[2] 这么 + Verb + 着 (zhème...zhe) — doing/thinking this way**
"这么+Verb+着" indicates "doing the verb in this way" or "thinking/feeling this way". The "着" indicates a continuous state.
① 大家都这么想来着。 (Everyone was thinking the same way.)
② 你别这么看着我。 (Dont look at me like that.)
③ 他一直这么想着，但没说出来。 (He kept thinking this way, but didnt say it out loud.)
**[3] 说起 (shuōqǐ) — speaking of; talking about**
"说起" means "to mention" or "to talk about". It introduces a topic or brings up a subject in conversation.
① 说起他来，我常常觉得他有点儿奇怪。 (Speaking of him, I often feel he is a bit strange.)
② 说起中国的美食，三天三夜也说不完。 (Speaking of Chinese cuisine — you could not finish talking about it in three days and nights.)
③ 一说到旅游，他就特别兴奋。 (Whenever the topic of travel comes up, he gets very excited.)
**[4] 光 (guāng) — only; merely; just**
"光" is an adverb meaning "only" or "just". It limits the scope of what is being talked about. Similar to "只" but more colloquial.
① 光听你说他的好话，他没有缺点吗？ (I only hear you say good things about him — doesnt he have any faults?)
② 光说不做可不行。 (Just talking without doing wont work.)
③ 光学习不休息，会累坏的。 (Only studying without rest — you will wear yourself out.)
**[5] 可不是嘛 (kě bú shì ma) — exactly!; you said it!**
"可不是嘛" is a colloquial expression used to strongly agree with what someone just said. Means "exactly!" or "you said it!"
① 可不是嘛！他学习特别认真。 (You said it! He studies very seriously.)
② 可不是嘛！今天真冷。 (Exactly! It is really cold today.)
③ 可不是嘛！我也这么觉得。 (You said it! I think so too.)
**[6] 说不定 (shuōbudìng) — maybe; perhaps; who knows**
"说不定" means "maybe", "perhaps", or "you never know". It expresses uncertainty about a future possibility. Can be placed at the beginning or middle of a sentence.
① 说不定他已经忘记了。 (Maybe he has already forgotten.)
② 明天说不定会下雨。 (It might rain tomorrow — you never know.)
③ 你好好儿解释，说不定她就能原谅你。 (If you explain it properly, maybe she will forgive you.)
