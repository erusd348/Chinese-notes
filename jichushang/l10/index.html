课文 3
小明10岁生日的时候,爸爸妈妈给他买了一台电脑。本来是为了帮他学习的,可没想到小明迷上了玩儿游戏,一玩儿就是好几个小时。爸爸妈妈说什么他都不听。最近的一次考试,小明的成绩下降了很多,而且他的视力也一天比一天差。爸爸妈妈很着急,决定不让他再玩儿了。不能玩儿游戏了,小明这两天急得饭也吃不香、觉也睡不好。看着儿子不说、不笑的样子,爸爸妈妈不知道怎么办才好。
📝 Notes
**[1] 怎么 + Verb + 都 + (不/没) — no matter how**
"怎么 + Verb + 都 + 不/没..." means "no matter how one tries, the result doesnt change". Expresses frustration that despite all efforts, something cant be achieved.
① 可像我这样的人怎么减肥都没用。 (But for someone like me, no matter how I try to lose weight, it doesnt work.)
② 他怎么学都学不会。 (No matter how hard he studies, he cant learn it.)
③ 这东西怎么修都修不好。 (No matter how you fix it, this thing just wont work.)
**[2] 谁/哪儿/什么/怎么 + 也/都 — universal reference**
An emphatic structure meaning "(no matter) who/where/what/how". The question word + "也/都" covers all possibilities.
① 谁也不知道。 (Nobody knows.)
② 我哪儿都不想去。 (I dont want to go anywhere.)
③ 他什么都能吃。 (He can eat anything.)
④ 怎么劝都没用。 (No matter how you persuade him, its useless.)
**[3] 太 + Adj + 了 (tài...le) — too; extremely**
"太 + Adjective + 了" means "too (adjective)", "extremely (adjective)", or "(adjective) to excess". Can be positive or negative depending on the adjective.
① 你说得太绝对了吧？ (What you said is too absolute, isnt it?)
② 太好了！ (Great! / Wonderful!)
③ 太多了，吃不了。 (Too much — I cant eat it all.)
**[4] 一天比一天 (yì tiān bǐ yì tiān) — day by day**
"一天比一天" means "day by day" or "more and more each day". Describes a progressive increase or decrease over time.
① 那你说为什么现在的胖子一天比一天多？ (Then tell me, why are there more and more fat people every day?)
② 天气一天比一天暖和了。 (The weather is getting warmer day by day.)
③ 他的汉语一天比一天进步。 (His Chinese is improving day by day.)
**[5] 既然 (jìrán) — since; now that**
"既然" introduces a reason/fact that is already established. The second clause explains the logical conclusion. "就" is often used in the second clause.
① 既然吃药也没用，那就算了。 (Since taking medicine doesnt work either, lets just forget it.)
② 既然你已经决定了，我就不劝你了。 (Since you have already decided, I wont try to persuade you.)
③ 既然大家都来了，我们就开始吧。 (Since everyones here, lets start.)
**[6] 万一 (wànyī) — just in case; in the event that**
"万一" introduces an unlikely but possible situation — something one hopes wont happen but prepares for just in case. Often used when warning or being cautious.
① 万一让我爱人知道了，就麻烦了。 (If my spouse finds out, it will be trouble.)
② 带上伞吧，万一下雨呢。 (Bring an umbrella, just in case it rains.)
③ 万一他不同意，我们怎么办？ (What if he doesnt agree? What should we do?)
**[7] 一 + Verb + 就是 + Quantity/Time — once started, it lasts for...**
This structure indicates that the action, once started, lasts for a surprisingly long time or involves a large quantity.
① 一玩儿就是好几个小时。 (Once he starts playing, it is for several hours straight.)
② 一睡就是半天。 (Once he falls asleep, it is for half a day.)
③ 一买就是一大堆。 (Once he starts buying, it is a whole pile of stuff.)
