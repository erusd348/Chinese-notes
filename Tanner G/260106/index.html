疑问词 + 都/也 + V. Q-word + dōu/yě + V. Structure “Wh-ever”: Questions words (谁, 什么, 哪儿, 什么时候) followed by “都” or “也” in a negative sentence means “anyone/anything/anywhere/anytime”.
只要... 就... zhǐyào... jiù... Structure “As long as... then...”: Expresses a sufficient condition.
💬 Sentences
我们一直庆祝到新年夜。 Wǒmen yīzhí qìngzhù dào Xīnnián Yè. We kept celebrating until New Year’s Eve.
我什么都没做。 Wǒ shénme dōu méi zuò. I didn‘t do anything.
他哪儿都去过。 Tā nǎr dōu qùguò. He has been everywhere.
他谁都认识。 Tā shéi dōu rènshi. He knows everyone.
他什么时候都很帅。 Tā shénme shíhòu dōu hěn shuài. He is handsome at all times / anytime.
他只有两件事不会，这也不会，那也不会。 Tā zhǐyǒu liǎng jiàn shì bú huì, zhè yě bú huì, nà yě bú huì. There are only two things he can‘t do: he can’t do this, and he can‘t do that either. (A humorous way to say he can’t do anything)
吃得苦中苦，方为人上人。 Chī dé kǔ zhōng kǔ, fāng wéi rén shàng rén. Only by enduring the hardest of hardships can one rise above the common lot. (Proverb)
只要能吃苦，就有吃不完的苦。 Zhǐyào néng chīkǔ, jiù yǒu chībùwán de kǔ. As long as you can endure hardship, there will be an endless amount of hardship for you to endure. (A modern cynical twist on the proverb)
